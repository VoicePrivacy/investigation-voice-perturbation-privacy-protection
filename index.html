Many-to-One Adversarial Speech Generation for Voice Privacy Protection

Authors: Liping Chen, Shihao Chen, Kong Aik Lee, Li-Rong Dai, Zhen-Hua Ling
Abstract: In this paper, we focus on hiding and obfuscating the speaker identities that can be perceived by speaker recognition modeling while preserving the perceptual speaker attributes. To this end, we resort to the speaker adversarial perturbation technique and propose a framework for adversarial speech generation. In our proposed framework, the many-to-one concept was applied for supervising speaker perturbation generation. That is, the speakers within all speech files are perceived to be a common unreal speaker by speaker models. Besides, speech frames select their corresponding perturbation according to the attributes within themselves. Our speaker identification and verification experiments carried out on VoxCeleb1 and 2 justified the effectiveness of the proposed method in misleading speaker identification decisions, hiding and obfuscating speaker identity in both white- and black-box evaluations and for both seen and unseen speakers. Moreover, its capability in perturbing diarization system was justified. As a preliminary work in voice privacy aimed at protecting speaker identity while preserving perceptual speaker attributes, this paper will establish the foundation for our future research. Here, we present samples generated using several adversarial perturbation generation methods proposed in the paper. In the context of the experiments, 'Recording' represents the original speech, 'MI-FGSM' denotes the adversarial samples generated using the MI-FGSM method, 'Feedforward' denotes the adversarial samples generated using the Feedforward method, 'Mean Only' signifies the results obtained solely using the Batch Mean loss function as presented in the paper, 'Angular Only' refers to the outcomes obtained solely using the Angular loss function proposed in the paper, while 'Proposed' represents the best results demonstrated in our paper. Due to the limitation of MI-FGSM in perturbing speech from unseen speakers, the samples presented here were selected from our curated in-set test dataset in Voxceleb1. We randomly selected 6 male and 6 female speakers from the dataset and generated corresponding adversarial samples using the four aforementioned methods.
